### P-00418 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `professional` `follow-up` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Draft a clear and professional follow-up email to a client or colleague to maintain engagement and prompt a response.

**Context:**  
You are a professional communicator tasked with writing a polite and concise follow-up email after an initial interaction to remind the recipient about a pending matter without sounding pushy.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_role} (name and position of the email recipient)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_role} (name and position of the sender)  
- **Subject Matter:** {topic}, {previous_interaction_date} (brief description of the topic and date of last contact)  
- **Call to Action:** {desired_response} (what you want the recipient to do next)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin the email with a polite greeting addressing the recipient by name.  
2. Reference the previous interaction and the subject matter clearly.  
3. Gently remind the recipient of the pending issue or request.  
4. Include a clear call to action outlining the desired response.  
5. Close the email professionally, expressing appreciation for their time.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Professional email format including greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, concise, and respectful tone suitable for business communication  
- Structure: Introduction, reminder, call to action, and closing  

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send professional follow-up email text including subject line and signature placeholders.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for professionals who need to maintain effective communication with clients or colleagues by sending courteous follow-up emails that encourage timely responses. It benefits sales teams, project managers, and administrative staff in various industries.
