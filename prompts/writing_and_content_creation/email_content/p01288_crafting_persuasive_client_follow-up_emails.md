### P-01288 - Crafting Persuasive Client Follow-Up Emails

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tags:** `email` `client-communication` `follow-up` `professional-writing`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose a clear, professional, and persuasive follow-up email to re-engage a client who has not responded to a previous outreach.

**Context:**  
You are a sales representative aiming to maintain strong client relationships and encourage a response without appearing intrusive or overly aggressive. The tone should balance professionalism with approachability to foster positive engagement.

**Input:**  
- **Client Details:** {client_name}, {client_company} (recipientâ€™s name and company)  
- **Previous Interaction Summary:** {last_contact_date}, {previous_topic} (date and subject of last communication)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {follow_up_reason} (main reason for reaching out again)  
- **Call-to-Action:** {desired_action} (specific action you want the client to take)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing {client_name} by name.  
2. Reference the last interaction on {last_contact_date} regarding {previous_topic} to establish context.  
3. Clearly state the reason for the follow-up using {follow_up_reason}, emphasizing value without pressure.  
4. Include a professional yet friendly call-to-action encouraging {desired_action}.  
5. Close with an invitation for further questions or discussion and a courteous sign-off.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: Between 300 and 500 words  
- Format: Formal email format with greeting, body paragraphs, and closing  
- Style: Professional, courteous, and persuasive without being pushy  
- Structure: Logical flow with clear introduction, body, and conclusion

**Output Format:**  
A fully composed follow-up email draft ready to be sent, formatted as standard professional correspondence including subject line suggestion.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for sales professionals, account managers, or customer success representatives who need to re-engage clients after no response. It helps maintain client relationships and increase reply rates by crafting thoughtful and respectful follow-up emails.
