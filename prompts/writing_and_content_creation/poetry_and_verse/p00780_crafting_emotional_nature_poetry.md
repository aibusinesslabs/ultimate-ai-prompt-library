### P-00780 - Crafting Emotional Nature Poetry

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Poetry & Verse  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `poetry` `nature` `emotion` `creative-writing`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose an original poem that expresses a specific emotion through vivid descriptions of a natural setting.

**Context:**  
You are a beginner poet aiming to explore emotional expression by connecting feelings to elements of nature. The goal is to create a short poem that evokes mood and imagery accessible to a broad audience.

**Input:**  
- **Emotion and Setting:** {emotion}, {natural_setting} (the primary feeling to convey and the natural environment to describe)  
- **Imagery Elements:** {sensory_details} (specific sights, sounds, smells, or textures associated with the setting)  
- **Tone and Mood:** {desired_tone} (the overall tone or mood you want the poem to have, e.g., calm, melancholic, joyful)  
- **Poetic Form:** {poem_style} (optional, e.g., free verse, haiku, quatrain)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin by reflecting on the chosen {emotion} and how it relates to the {natural_setting}.  
2. Incorporate at least three {sensory_details} to create vivid imagery that supports the emotion.  
3. Use language and literary devices (such as metaphor or simile) to enhance the {desired_tone}.  
4. Follow the {poem_style} format, if specified; otherwise, write in free verse.  
5. Ensure the poem is concise, expressive, and accessible for a beginner audience.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Plain text poem with line breaks  
- Style: Evocative, clear, and emotionally resonant  
- Structure: Optional adherence to specified poetic form; must include sensory imagery supporting the emotion

**Output Format:**  
An original poem in plain text that meets the above criteria, formatted with appropriate line breaks and stanza separations if applicable.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for beginner writers and poetry students seeking to develop emotional expression through nature imagery. It supports creative exploration in educational settings, workshops, or personal writing practice.
