### P-01018 - Crafting a Persuasive Client Follow-Up Email

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tags:** `email` `client-communication` `follow-up` `professional-writing`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a professional, persuasive follow-up email to re-engage a client who has not responded to previous communications.

**Context:**  
You are a business development manager aiming to maintain a positive relationship and encourage a response from a prospective client who has shown initial interest but has recently gone silent.

**Input:**  
- **Client Details:** {client_name}, {client_company} (Name and company of the recipient)  
- **Previous Interaction Summary:** {previous_meeting_date}, {topics_discussed} (Date of last contact and key discussion points)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {follow_up_objective} (Main goal, e.g., schedule a call, provide additional information, close the deal)  
- **Tone and Style Preferences:** {tone} (e.g., friendly, formal, assertive)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a courteous greeting addressing {client_name} by name.  
2. Reference the previous interaction on {previous_meeting_date} and briefly summarize the key discussion points ({topics_discussed}) to remind the client.  
3. Clearly state the purpose of the follow-up based on {follow_up_objective}, emphasizing the value or benefits relevant to {client_company}.  
4. Encourage a response by proposing a specific next step, such as scheduling a call or meeting.  
5. Close the email with a professional and polite sign-off aligned with the {tone} specified.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 300-500 words  
- Format: Single coherent email body without bullet points or numbered lists  
- Style: Professional and persuasive, matching the {tone} input  
- Structure: Introduction, context reminder, clear call-to-action, polite closing

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send follow-up email tailored to the inputs, formatted as plain text.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to professionally re-engage clients who have become unresponsive after initial contact. Ideal for sales professionals, account managers, or business developers aiming to nurture leads and advance client relationships with polished, effective email communication.
