### P-00959 - Crafting a Compelling Multi-Act Screenplay Outline

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Scripts & Screenwriting  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Tags:** `screenplay` `structure` `character development` `plot outline`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Develop a detailed multi-act screenplay outline that clearly defines key plot points, character arcs, and dramatic tension tailored to a specific genre and theme.

**Context:**  
You are a professional screenwriter tasked with creating a structured blueprint for a feature-length screenplay. Your goal is to ensure the story is engaging, well-paced, and adheres to classic screenplay principles, focusing on character motivations and thematic depth.

**Input:**  
- **Story Elements:** {genre}, {theme} (the genre of the screenplay and underlying thematic message)  
- **Characters:** {protagonist_name}, {protagonist_goal}, {antagonist_name}, {antagonist_goal} (main characters and their primary objectives)  
- **Setting & Tone:** {setting}, {tone} (time period, location, and overall mood or atmosphere)  
- **Plot Requirements:** {key_plot_points} (specific plot points or story beats that must be included)

**Instructions:**  
1. Outline the screenplay in a three-act structure: Setup, Confrontation, and Resolution.  
2. Identify major plot points including inciting incident, midpoint, climax, and resolution.  
3. Illustrate how the protagonist’s and antagonist’s goals create conflict and drive the narrative forward.  
4. Integrate the thematic elements naturally into character decisions and plot developments.  
5. Ensure the setting and tone reinforce the mood and support the story’s emotional impact.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 500-750 words  
- Format: Clear, bulleted or numbered outline with distinct act divisions  
- Style: Professional, concise, and focused on cinematic storytelling principles  
- Structure: Must follow a classic three-act screenplay outline format with explicit plot and character descriptions

**Output Format:**  
A structured screenplay outline segmented by acts, including labeled plot points, character arcs, and thematic notes. Use clear headings for each act and subpoints for plot developments and conflicts.

#### Use Case:  
Ideal for screenwriters preparing a first draft or pitching a screenplay concept to producers. This prompt helps distill complex story ideas into a coherent, professionally formatted outline that highlights dramatic structure and character dynamics for film development purposes.
