### P-00568 - Polished Professional Follow-Up Email

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a concise and professional follow-up email to a client or colleague to maintain engagement and encourage a response.

**Context:**  
You are a professional communicator tasked with drafting a polite and effective follow-up email that reflects professionalism, reinforces the original message, and prompts a reply without sounding pushy.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_role} (name and job title of the email recipient)  
- **Original Message Summary:** {summary} (brief overview of the previous communication or reason for follow-up)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_role} (your name and position)  
- **Call to Action:** {desired_action} (specific action you want the recipient to take)

**Instructions:**  
1. Address the recipient politely using their name and appropriate title.  
2. Briefly reference the previous communication to provide context.  
3. Clearly state the purpose of the follow-up in a courteous tone.  
4. Include a call to action that encourages the recipient to respond or take the desired step.  
5. Close the email professionally with your name and contact information.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Standard professional email format with greeting, body, and signature  
- Style: Polite, clear, and professional tone  
- Structure: One introductory paragraph, one body paragraph referencing the previous message and purpose, one closing paragraph with call to action and sign-off

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send email text including greeting, body, and closing signature.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt to create effective follow-up emails that maintain professional relationships and drive timely responses from clients or colleagues. Ideal for beginners learning to communicate clearly and courteously in business settings.
