### P-00480 - Crafting a Themed Inspirational Poem

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Poetry & Verse  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `poetry` `creative writing` `inspiration` `beginner`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write an original, inspirational poem based on a specific theme and tone provided.

**Context:**  
You are a creative poet tasked with composing an uplifting and motivational poem that resonates emotionally with readers. The poem should be accessible to a broad audience and evoke positive feelings.

**Input:**  
- **Theme and Tone**: {theme}, {tone} (e.g., theme: "hope," tone: "encouraging")  
- **Target Audience**: {audience} (description of who the poem is intended for, e.g., "young adults")  
- **Key Imagery or Symbols**: {imagery} (specific images or symbols to include, e.g., "sunrise," "mountains")  
- **Preferred Length**: {length} (desired word count within 150-300 words)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin by understanding the provided {theme} and {tone} to set the emotional foundation of the poem.  
2. Use language and phrasing suitable for the {audience}, ensuring clarity and relatability.  
3. Incorporate the {imagery} to create vivid and engaging scenes within the poem.  
4. Compose the poem within the specified {length} to maintain focus and impact.  
5. Review the poem for flow, coherence, and adherence to the inspirational tone.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: Between 150 and 300 words  
- Format: Free verse or rhymed verse with consistent style throughout  
- Style: Inspirational and uplifting language, accessible vocabulary  
- Structure: Single poem, no stanzas required but allowed if stylistically appropriate

**Output Format:**  
Provide the completed poem as plain text, titled with the {theme} capitalized.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for writers, educators, or content creators seeking to generate original inspirational poetry quickly and effectively. It helps beginners develop their poetic voice while delivering emotionally resonant content tailored to specific audiences and themes.
