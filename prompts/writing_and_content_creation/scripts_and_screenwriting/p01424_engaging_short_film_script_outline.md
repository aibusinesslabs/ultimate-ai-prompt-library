### P-01424 - Engaging Short Film Script Outline

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Scripts & Screenwriting  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `scriptwriting` `shortfilm` `storystructure` `creativewriting`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Create a concise and engaging outline for a short film script based on given themes and characters.

**Context:**  
You are a beginner screenwriter tasked with developing a clear and structured short film script outline that captures key story elements and flow for production guidance.

**Input:**  
- **Story Details:** {theme}, {setting} (core theme and location of the story)  
- **Characters:** {protagonist}, {antagonist} (main characters with brief descriptions)  
- **Plot Points:** {inciting_incident}, {climax} (two critical story events)  
- **Tone and Style:** {tone} (emotional atmosphere and writing style)

**Instructions:**  
1. Summarize the main idea incorporating the {theme} and {setting}.  
2. Introduce the {protagonist} and {antagonist} clearly, highlighting their motivations.  
3. Outline the storyâ€™s beginning with the {inciting_incident} that triggers the plot.  
4. Develop the progression leading to the {climax}, emphasizing conflict and stakes.  
5. Conclude with a brief resolution or open-ended note consistent with the {tone}.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Outline format with clear headings and bullet points  
- Style: Professional, concise, and accessible for beginner screenwriters  
- Structure: Logical flow following basic three-act structure elements

**Output Format:**  
A structured script outline with headings for Theme & Setting, Characters, Plot Development, and Resolution, formatted for easy reading and quick reference.

#### Use Case:  
Ideal for novice writers preparing a short film treatment or pitch. This prompt helps organize ideas clearly and communicate story essentials for collaboration or early-stage script development.
