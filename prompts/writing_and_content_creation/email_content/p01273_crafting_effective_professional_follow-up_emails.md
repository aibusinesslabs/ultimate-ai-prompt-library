### P-01273 - Crafting Effective Professional Follow-Up Emails

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tags:** `email` `professional` `follow-up` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose a polished, professional follow-up email to a business contact, maintaining a courteous tone while prompting a response or action.

**Context:**  
You are an experienced business communicator tasked with drafting a follow-up email to a contact you previously engaged with. The goal is to gently remind the recipient of your prior communication and encourage timely engagement without sounding pushy.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_title} (Name and professional title of the email recipient)  
- **Previous Interaction:** {previous_message_summary}, {date_of_last_contact} (Brief summary of last communication and date)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {follow_up_reason} (Reason for following up, e.g., awaiting response, scheduling a meeting)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_title}, {sender_company} (Your full name, job title, and company name)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing the recipient by name and title.  
2. Reference the previous interaction briefly, including the date and summary to provide context.  
3. Clearly state the reason for the follow-up and express your interest in continuing the conversation or receiving a response.  
4. Maintain a professional and courteous tone, avoiding any language that could be perceived as demanding or impatient.  
5. Close the email with a polite call to action and a professional sign-off including your name and contact information.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 300-500 words  
- Format: Formal email structure with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Professional, courteous, clear, and concise  
- Structure: Logical flow with introduction, context reminder, follow-up purpose, and closing statement

**Output Format:**  
A complete professional follow-up email draft ready to be sent, formatted as standard business correspondence.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to send a professional follow-up email to clients, partners, or colleagues after an initial outreach or meeting. It is ideal for maintaining positive business relationships and encouraging timely responses without causing discomfort or pressure.
