### P-01048 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Create a concise and professional follow-up email to re-engage a client or colleague after an initial meeting or correspondence.

**Context:**  
You are a professional communication assistant tasked with drafting a polite and effective follow-up email that maintains a courteous tone while encouraging a response or next steps.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_position} (Name and role of the recipient)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_position} (Name and role of the sender)  
- **Initial Interaction:** {meeting_date}, {topic_discussed} (Date of last interaction and main subject)  
- **Purpose:** {follow_up_reason} (Reason for following up, e.g., scheduling a meeting, requesting feedback)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing {recipient_name}.  
2. Reference the initial interaction by mentioning {meeting_date} and {topic_discussed}.  
3. Clearly state the purpose of the follow-up using {follow_up_reason}.  
4. Encourage a response or propose next steps in a courteous manner.  
5. Close the email professionally with sender details and a call to action.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Formal email structure with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, professional, and concise  
- Structure: Logical flow from greeting to closing with clear purpose articulation

**Output Format:**  
A complete follow-up email draft ready to be sent, including greeting, body paragraphs, and closing signature.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt to generate professional follow-up emails that help maintain strong business relationships and improve response rates. Ideal for professionals seeking to re-engage contacts after meetings or initial outreach.
