### P-00703 - Professional Follow-Up Email Draft

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a professional and courteous follow-up email to a client or colleague to check on the status of a previous request or proposal.

**Context:**  
You are a business professional aiming to maintain positive communication and prompt a timely response without seeming pushy. The tone should be polite, clear, and respectful.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Information:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_email} (name and email of the person receiving the email)  
- **Previous Interaction Details:** {previous_subject}, {previous_date} (subject and date of the last communication or proposal)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {follow_up_reason} (brief reason for follow-up, e.g., status update, decision request)  
- **Sender Information:** {sender_name}, {sender_position}, {sender_company} (your name, job title, and company)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing {recipient_name}.  
2. Reference the previous interaction by mentioning {previous_subject} and {previous_date}.  
3. Clearly state the purpose of your follow-up using {follow_up_reason}, maintaining a courteous tone.  
4. Offer assistance or provide your availability for any further discussion.  
5. Close the email professionally with your name, position, and company.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Standard email format with greeting, body, closing, and signature  
- Style: Professional, polite, and clear without being overly formal or casual  
- Structure: Organized in short paragraphs for easy readability

**Output Format:**  
A complete follow-up email draft ready to be sent, including greeting, body paragraphs, closing remarks, and signature.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for professionals who need to send timely follow-up emails to clients or colleagues after a meeting, proposal, or request. It helps maintain engagement and professionalism while encouraging prompt responses without pressure.
