### P-01390 - Launch Announcement Press Release

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Press Releases  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `press release` `product launch` `public relations` `marketing`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a professional press release announcing the launch of a new product or service.

**Context:**  
You are a public relations specialist tasked with creating a clear and engaging press release to inform media outlets and potential customers about a new product or service launch by a company.

**Input:**  
- **Company Details:** {company_name}, {industry} (the organization launching the product/service)  
- **Product Information:** {product_name}, {product_description} (name and brief overview of the product/service)  
- **Launch Details:** {launch_date}, {availability} (date of launch and where/how the product will be available)  
- **Contact Information:** {contact_name}, {contact_email} (media contact for follow-up inquiries)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a strong headline that highlights the product launch.  
2. Write a compelling opening paragraph summarizing the key announcement.  
3. Provide details about the product/service and its benefits.  
4. Include launch date and availability information clearly.  
5. End with contact details and a call to action for media follow-up.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Formal press release style with headline and body text  
- Style: Professional, clear, and concise language suitable for media distribution  
- Structure: Headline, opening paragraph, body paragraphs, closing with contact info

**Output Format:**  
A polished press release text ready for distribution, including headline and contact section.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt to quickly generate effective press releases for new product launches, helping PR teams or small business owners communicate important news professionally and efficiently to journalists and customers. Ideal for startups, marketing agencies, and communications departments.
