### P-00735 - Crafting a Thematic Poem for Beginners

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Poetry & Verse  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `poetry` `creative-writing` `theme` `beginner`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose a short original poem that captures a specific theme and evokes emotion, suitable for a beginner poet.

**Context:**  
You are an aspiring poet seeking to develop your skills by writing concise, expressive poems centered around clear themes. Your goal is to create engaging verse that is accessible yet meaningful.

**Input:**  
- **Theme and Tone:** {theme}, {tone} (the central topic and emotional atmosphere of the poem)  
- **Poem Type:** {poem_type} (e.g., free verse, haiku, limerick)  
- **Imagery Keywords:** {imagery_keywords} (3-5 descriptive words or phrases to inspire vivid images)  
- **Target Audience:** {audience} (intended readers such as children, adults, or general audience)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin by reflecting on the {theme} and the desired {tone} to set the mood.  
2. Choose a {poem_type} that best suits the theme and beginner skill level.  
3. Incorporate the {imagery_keywords} as sensory elements to create vivid mental pictures.  
4. Write a poem between 150 and 300 words that is clear, expressive, and emotionally resonant.  
5. Ensure the poem is appropriate and engaging for the specified {audience}.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Poem with line breaks appropriate to the chosen {poem_type}  
- Style: Simple, clear language with emotional depth suitable for beginners  
- Structure: Follow the conventions of the specified {poem_type} without complex poetic devices  

**Output Format:**  
A complete poem presented with appropriate line breaks and stanza structure, clearly reflecting the chosen theme, tone, and imagery, tailored to the target audience.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for beginner poets training to express themes through poetry with guidance on style and imagery. It benefits educators, creative writing students, and hobbyists aiming to develop foundational verse writing skills in a structured yet creative manner.
