### P-00103 - Professional Client Follow-Up Email Generator

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tags:** `email` `client-communication` `follow-up` `professional-writing`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Generate a polished, professional follow-up email to a client after an initial meeting or proposal presentation, aimed at reinforcing interest and encouraging progression to the next step.

**Context:**  
You are a professional business development manager seeking to maintain strong client relationships by sending clear, courteous, and persuasive follow-up emails that summarize key points, express appreciation, and outline next steps.

**Input:**  
- **Client Details:** {client_name}, {client_company} (recipient’s name and company)  
- **Meeting Summary:** {meeting_date}, {key_discussion_points} (date of meeting and main topics discussed)  
- **Your Contact Information:** {your_name}, {your_position}, {your_company} (sender’s full name, job title, and company)  
- **Call to Action:** {desired_next_step}, {deadline_or_timeline} (preferred next action and any relevant timeline)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a courteous greeting addressing {client_name} personally.  
2. Express gratitude for the meeting held on {meeting_date} and briefly summarize {key_discussion_points} discussed.  
3. Reaffirm your company’s value proposition and how it aligns with {client_company}’s needs.  
4. Clearly state the {desired_next_step} you would like to pursue and mention {deadline_or_timeline} if applicable.  
5. Close professionally with your full contact details and an invitation for any questions or further discussion.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 300-500 words  
- Format: Formal business email structure with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Professional, courteous, and persuasive tone without jargon  
- Structure: Logical flow with clear paragraphs, concise sentences, and a strong call to action  

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send follow-up email in plain text format, including subject line suggestion.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to craft a follow-up email after client meetings or proposal presentations to maintain engagement, demonstrate professionalism, and encourage timely responses. Ideal for sales professionals, account managers, and consultants aiming to strengthen client relationships.
