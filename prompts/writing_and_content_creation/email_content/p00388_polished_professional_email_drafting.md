### P-00388 - Polished Professional Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `professional` `business` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose a clear, concise, and professional email based on the provided details to effectively communicate the intended message.

**Context:**  
You are an experienced business communicator tasked with drafting an email that reflects professionalism and clarity, suitable for a corporate environment.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_title} (name and position of the email recipient)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_title} (name and position of the sender)  
- **Email Purpose:** {purpose} (brief description of the reason for the email)  
- **Key Points:** {key_points} (main topics or information that must be included in the email)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin the email with a polite and appropriate greeting using the recipient’s name and title.  
2. Introduce yourself briefly and state the purpose of the email clearly within the first two sentences.  
3. Elaborate on the key points logically, ensuring each point is addressed clearly and professionally.  
4. Use polite and formal language throughout the email, avoiding jargon and overly complex sentences.  
5. Close the email with a courteous sign-off, including the sender’s name and title.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Standard professional email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Formal and respectful tone suitable for business communication  
- Structure: Clear introduction, body with key points, and professional closing

**Output Format:**  
A fully composed professional email as a plain text block ready for sending.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to draft professional emails for business purposes, such as making requests, providing updates, or initiating contact. Ideal for professionals and beginners aiming to improve their corporate communication skills.
