### P-01364 - Crafting a Compelling Short Film Script Outline

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Scripts & Screenwriting  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Tags:** `screenwriting` `shortfilm` `storystructure` `scriptoutline`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Create a detailed outline for a short film script that effectively establishes characters, setting, conflict, and resolution within a concise narrative framework.

**Context:**  
You are an experienced screenwriter tasked with developing a structured short film outline. Your goal is to help filmmakers visualize the story’s progression clearly and ensure the narrative is engaging and coherent, suitable for a 10-15 minute film.

**Input:**  
- **Story Elements:** {protagonist}, {antagonist}, {setting} (main characters and location)  
- **Plot Details:** {central_conflict}, {key_events} (primary conflict and major plot points)  
- **Tone and Genre:** {genre}, {tone} (style and mood of the story)  
- **Additional Notes:** {theme}, {special_considerations} (underlying message and any unique storytelling aspects)

**Instructions:**  
1. Introduce the protagonist and antagonist, briefly describing their motivations and roles.  
2. Establish the setting, emphasizing how it influences the story’s mood and action.  
3. Outline the central conflict clearly, indicating what drives the narrative tension.  
4. Break down the plot into 3-5 key events or scenes that progress the story logically towards resolution.  
5. Conclude with a resolution that reflects the theme and leaves a clear narrative closure.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 300-500 words  
- Format: Structured outline with clear headings for each section  
- Style: Professional, concise, and engaging  
- Structure: Logical flow following standard short film narrative progression (Setup, Conflict, Climax, Resolution)

**Output Format:**  
A well-organized script outline divided into sections: Characters, Setting, Conflict, Key Events, and Resolution, presented in paragraph or bullet format for easy reference.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for screenwriters, filmmakers, and content creators who need a focused, clear short film blueprint before writing the full script. It assists in pre-production planning by ensuring the story’s core elements are thoughtfully developed and aligned with the desired tone and genre.
