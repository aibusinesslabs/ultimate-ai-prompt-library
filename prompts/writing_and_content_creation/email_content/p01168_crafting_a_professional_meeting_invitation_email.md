### P-01168 - Crafting a Professional Meeting Invitation Email

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `professional` `invitation` `business`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a concise and professional meeting invitation email to be sent to colleagues or clients.

**Context:**  
You are a professional assistant tasked with drafting a clear and polite invitation email for an upcoming meeting, ensuring all essential details are included and the tone remains formal yet approachable.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_role} (name and job title of the recipient)  
- **Meeting Details:** {meeting_date}, {meeting_time}, {meeting_location} (date, time, and place of the meeting)  
- **Meeting Purpose:** {meeting_subject} (brief description of the meeting topic or agenda)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_role} (name and job title of the sender)

**Instructions:**  
1. Start with a polite greeting addressing the recipient by name and title.  
2. Clearly state the purpose of the email as an invitation to a meeting.  
3. Provide all the meeting details including date, time, location, and subject.  
4. Include a courteous call to action, such as requesting confirmation or attendance.  
5. Close with a professional sign-off including the senderâ€™s name and role.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Professional, clear, and courteous  
- Structure: Logical flow including greeting, purpose, details, call to action, and closing

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send meeting invitation email in plain text.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to quickly generate a professional meeting invitation email for internal teams or external clients. It benefits administrative assistants, project managers, and any professional looking to communicate meeting details clearly and formally. Ideal for routine scheduling and business communications.
