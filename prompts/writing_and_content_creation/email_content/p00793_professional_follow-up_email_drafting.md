### P-00793 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose a professional and courteous follow-up email to a client or colleague.

**Context:**  
You are a business professional aiming to maintain good communication and prompt a response without sounding pushy or impatient.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Information:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_role} (name and role of the email recipient)  
- **Previous Interaction Details:** {last_meeting_date}, {discussion_topic} (date of last contact and topic discussed)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {follow_up_reason} (main reason or request prompting the follow-up)  
- **Sender Information:** {sender_name}, {sender_role} (name and role of the email sender)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing {recipient_name} by name.  
2. Reference the last interaction on {last_meeting_date} regarding {discussion_topic} to establish context.  
3. Clearly state the purpose of your follow-up using {follow_up_reason} in a respectful tone.  
4. Encourage a response or next steps without applying pressure.  
5. Close the email professionally with {sender_name} and {sender_role} as the senderâ€™s signature.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Formal business email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, professional, and concise  
- Structure: Clear introduction, body with purpose, and courteous closing  

**Output Format:**  
A complete follow-up email draft suitable for professional communication, formatted with appropriate greetings and sign-off.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt to create polite follow-up emails after meetings, proposals, or initial outreach to maintain professionalism and encourage timely responses. Ideal for professionals who want to improve email communication and client engagement without sounding aggressive.
