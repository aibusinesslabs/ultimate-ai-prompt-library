### P-01349 - Crafting a Compelling Feature Film Script Outline

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Scripts & Screenwriting  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Tags:** `screenwriting` `feature_film` `story_structure` `character_development`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Develop a detailed, professional outline for a feature-length film script that effectively integrates narrative structure, character arcs, and thematic elements.

**Context:**  
You are an expert screenwriter hired to create a foundational script outline for a new feature film project. Your goal is to provide a clear, engaging blueprint that balances plot progression, character development, and thematic depth, suitable for pitching or further scriptwriting.

**Input:**  
- **Story Elements:** {{genre}}, {{primary_theme}} (The genre of the film and the central theme driving the narrative)  
- **Characters:** {{protagonist_description}}, {{antagonist_description}} (Brief descriptions including motivations, flaws, and goals for the main characters)  
- **Setting & Tone:** {{setting_description}}, {{tone_description}} (Details about the film’s time, place, and overall mood or style)  
- **Plot Requirements:** {{key_plot_points}} (A list or summary of essential plot events or turning points to include)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin by outlining the three-act structure, clearly defining the Setup, Confrontation, and Resolution phases.  
2. Develop detailed summaries of key scenes or sequences within each act that advance the plot and reveal character growth.  
3. Integrate the protagonist’s and antagonist’s arcs, showing how their motivations and conflicts evolve throughout the story.  
4. Incorporate the primary theme consistently, illustrating how it influences character decisions and plot outcomes.  
5. Ensure the setting and tone are reflected in the narrative style and scene descriptions, establishing a cohesive atmosphere.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 500-750 words  
- Format: Structured outline with clear headings for each act and sections for characters, setting, and theme integration  
- Style: Professional, concise, and engaging, suitable for use by screenwriters and producers  
- Structure: Logical flow following classic screenplay storytelling conventions; avoid screenplay formatting, focus on outline clarity

**Output Format:**  
A well-organized, multi-section outline document, including:  
- Title and logline (one-sentence summary)  
- Three-act breakdown with act-specific scene summaries  
- Character arc descriptions tied to plot points  
- Thematic and tonal notes integrated throughout

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when developing a foundational script outline to pitch a feature film project or provide a clear roadmap for full scriptwriting. Ideal for screenwriters, producers, and creative teams seeking to align on story structure and character development before drafting the screenplay.
