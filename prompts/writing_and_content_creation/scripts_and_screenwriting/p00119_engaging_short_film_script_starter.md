### P-00119 - Engaging Short Film Script Starter

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Scripts & Screenwriting  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `screenwriting` `shortfilm` `scriptwriting` `creativewriting`  

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Create a concise and engaging opening scene for a short film script that introduces the main character, setting, and central conflict.

**Context:**  
You are a novice screenwriter crafting the first scene of a short film. The goal is to hook the audience quickly with vivid descriptions and compelling character motivation while adhering to basic screenplay formatting.

**Input:**  
- **Character Details:** {character_name}, {character_age}, {character_trait} (main characterâ€™s name, age, and defining personality trait)  
- **Setting:** {location}, {time_of_day} (where and when the story begins)  
- **Conflict:** {central_conflict} (the main problem or challenge the character faces)  
- **Tone & Genre:** {tone}, {genre} (overall mood and genre of the short film)  

**Instructions:**  
1. Write a script opening scene (approx. 150-300 words) following standard screenplay convention (scene heading, action lines, and character dialogue if applicable).  
2. Introduce the main character with relevant traits that influence the story.  
3. Establish the setting clearly to ground the audience in time and place.  
4. Present the central conflict in a way that creates immediate tension or curiosity.  
5. Use the specified tone and genre to guide the style and mood of the scene.  

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Basic screenplay format (scene heading, action, dialogue)  
- Style: Clear, engaging, appropriate to specified tone and genre  
- Structure: Must include an opening scene only, no additional scenes or exposition  

**Output Format:**  
A screenplay-formatted opening scene of a short film script, ready for further development.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for beginner screenwriters seeking to practice crafting gripping opening scenes that capture audience interest quickly. It helps generate structured and formatted script content for short films across various genres and tones.
