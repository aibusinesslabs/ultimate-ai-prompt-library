### P-01063 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a professional follow-up email to a client or colleague to remind them about a previously discussed topic or pending action.

**Context:**  
You are a professional communicator tasked with crafting a polite and clear follow-up email that maintains a courteous tone and encourages a response without appearing pushy.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_role} (Name and role of the email recipient)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_role} (Name and role of the email sender)  
- **Subject & Topic:** {email_subject}, {discussion_topic} (The email subject line and the main topic or action to follow up on)  
- **Previous Interaction:** {previous_date}, {previous_summary} (Date and brief summary of the last communication)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting using the recipient’s name.  
2. Reference the previous interaction by mentioning the date and topic.  
3. Clearly and courteously remind the recipient about the pending action or information.  
4. Encourage a response or next step without pressuring.  
5. Close with a professional sign-off including the sender’s name and role.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Formal email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, professional, and concise  
- Structure: Introduction, reminder body, call to action, and closing

**Output Format:**  
A complete, polished follow-up email ready to be sent, formatted with a subject line, greeting, body paragraphs, and closing signature.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to send a professional follow-up email that balances friendliness with professionalism. Ideal for business communications where maintaining good relationships is important while prompting timely responses.
