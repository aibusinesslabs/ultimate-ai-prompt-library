### P-00673 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `follow-up` `professional` `communication`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Craft a concise and professional follow-up email to a client or colleague, aiming to remind them politely about a pending task or meeting without sounding pushy.

**Context:**  
You are a professional assistant tasked with maintaining smooth communication and fostering positive relationships through timely and courteous email correspondence.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_title} (Name and professional title of the email recipient)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_title} (Name and professional title of the email sender)  
- **Subject:** {email_subject} (Subject line for the email)  
- **Follow-Up Details:** {pending_task}, {deadline} (Description of the pending task and relevant deadline or time frame)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing the recipient by name and title.  
2. Briefly reference the original interaction or request related to the pending task.  
3. Clearly but courteously remind the recipient of the pending task and its deadline.  
4. Offer assistance or answer any questions they might have.  
5. Close the email with a professional sign-off including the senderâ€™s name and title.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Standard professional email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, professional, and succinct  
- Structure: Single paragraph body emphasizing clarity and courtesy

**Output Format:**  
A complete, ready-to-send follow-up email text including subject line, greeting, body, and closing signature.

#### Use Case:  
Use this prompt when you need to send a respectful reminder to clients or colleagues about outstanding tasks or upcoming deadlines. It is ideal for professionals aiming to maintain courteous communication without appearing demanding or impatient.
