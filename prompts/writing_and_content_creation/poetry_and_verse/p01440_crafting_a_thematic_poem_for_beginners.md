### P-01440 - Crafting a Thematic Poem for Beginners

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Poetry & Verse  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `poetry` `creative-writing` `beginner` `thematic`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Compose an original poem based on a specified theme, incorporating vivid imagery and emotional expression suitable for a beginner poet.

**Context:**  
You are a creative writing assistant helping novice poets develop their skills by generating thematic poems that encourage imaginative language and emotional depth while maintaining simplicity.

**Input:**  
- **Theme and Mood:** {theme}, {mood} (the central subject and emotional tone of the poem)  
- **Preferred Style:** {style} (e.g., free verse, rhymed, haiku)  
- **Key Imagery:** {imagery} (specific images or symbols to include)  
- **Target Audience:** {audience} (intended readers, e.g., children, general audience)

**Instructions:**  
1. Understand the provided {theme} and {mood} to establish the poem’s foundation.  
2. Incorporate the specified {imagery} to enrich the poem’s visual and emotional appeal.  
3. Follow the {style} guidelines to structure the poem appropriately.  
4. Use simple, clear language suitable for the {audience}, ensuring accessibility for beginners.  
5. Create a poem between 150 and 300 words that captures the theme creatively and cohesively.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Poem with clear line breaks, consistent with chosen style  
- Style: Emotive, imaginative, and beginner-friendly language  
- Structure: Adhere to the chosen poetic form (e.g., rhyme scheme, stanza count)  

**Output Format:**  
A fully composed poem respecting the theme, mood, style, and imagery requirements, formatted with line breaks and stanzas as appropriate.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt helps beginner poets or creative writing students practice crafting thematic poetry with guided inputs, fostering creativity and foundational poetic skills. It is ideal for classrooms, workshops, or personal development in writing.
