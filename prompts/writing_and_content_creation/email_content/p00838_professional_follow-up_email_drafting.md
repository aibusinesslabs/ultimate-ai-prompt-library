### P-00838 - Professional Follow-Up Email Drafting

**Category:** Writing & Content Creation  
**Subcategory:** Email Content  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Tags:** `email` `professional` `follow-up` `business`

#### Prompt:

**Task:**  
Write a clear, polite, and professional follow-up email to a business contact who has not responded to a previous message.

**Context:**  
You are a professional communicator tasked with maintaining positive business relationships and encouraging timely responses through effective email follow-ups.

**Input:**  
- **Recipient Details:** {recipient_name}, {recipient_position} (name and job title of the email recipient)  
- **Previous Interaction:** {previous_email_date}, {previous_email_subject} (date and subject of the last email sent)  
- **Purpose of Follow-Up:** {reason_for_follow_up} (brief reason for following up)  
- **Sender Details:** {sender_name}, {sender_position} (name and position of the email sender)

**Instructions:**  
1. Begin with a polite greeting addressing {recipient_name}.  
2. Reference the previous email sent on {previous_email_date} with the subject "{previous_email_subject}".  
3. Clearly state the reason for the follow-up, incorporating {reason_for_follow_up}.  
4. Encourage a response by expressing openness to assist or provide additional information.  
5. Close professionally with a courteous sign-off including {sender_name} and {sender_position}.

**Constraints:**  
- Length: 150-300 words  
- Format: Standard professional email format with greeting, body, and closing  
- Style: Polite, concise, and respectful tone suitable for business communication  
- Structure: Logical flow including introduction, purpose reminder, call to action, and closing

**Output Format:**  
A complete, formatted professional follow-up email text ready to send.

#### Use Case:  
This prompt is ideal for professionals needing to write courteous follow-up emails that maintain rapport and prompt timely communication. It benefits salespeople, project managers, and anyone in business correspondence seeking effective email follow-ups after no response.
